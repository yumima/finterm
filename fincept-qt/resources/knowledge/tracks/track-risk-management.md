# Path: Risk Management Essentials

6 entries on sizing, stops, drawdown control, and surviving the bad years that make the good years matter.

Estimated time: **3–5 hours of reading**, plus rigorous trade journaling.

> The math of compounding is unforgiving: a 50% drawdown requires +100% to recover. Spend more time avoiding drawdowns than picking winners.

## What "done" looks like

You have a written risk budget per trade, a max-drawdown threshold that triggers de-risking, and a pre-committed crash-response plan. You can articulate your edge in R-multiples and explain why fractional Kelly is what you size at.
