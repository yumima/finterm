# Path: Building Your First Portfolio

10 steps from "I have $10k and a brokerage account" to a balanced, monitored, sane portfolio you understand. For complete beginners.

Estimated time: **6–10 hours over a few weekends**, then quarterly maintenance.

## Foundations to read (3–5)

4. [[fcf|Free Cash Flow]] — the most-honest profitability measure
5. [[pe-ratio|P/E Ratio]] — the most common multiple

## Build the buying list (6–7)

6. [[build-a-watchlist|How to Build a Watchlist]] — funnel from idea to actionable

## Buying with discipline (8–9)

8. [[size-a-position|How to Size a Position]] — risk per trade, not gut size
9. [[build-a-thesis|Build a Thesis]] — write it down BEFORE buying

## Surviving the bad days (10)

10. [[handle-a-crash|How to Handle a Market Crash]] — pre-commit to behavior under stress

## What "done" looks like

You have a written investment policy (allocation, rebalancing rules, position limits, drawdown thresholds). You have 10–25 holdings with a thesis you can defend for each. You know your max drawdown tolerance and have cash buffer outside the portfolio. You spend more time reading 10-Ks than checking prices.
