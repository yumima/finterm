# Path: Value Investing

A 10-entry sequence to learn the basics of fundamental value investing — from reading a balance sheet to writing a thesis you can defend.

Estimated time: **5–8 hours of focused reading**, plus practice on real companies.

## Foundation — understand the business numerically (1–3)

1. [[reading-financials|Reading Company Financial Statements]] — the three statements + how they tie together
2. [[fcf|Free Cash Flow]] — what's actually left for shareholders
3. [[ebitda|EBITDA]] — operating profitability proxy + its dangers

## Core ratios — pricing the business (4–6)

4. [[pe-ratio|P/E Ratio]] — the most common multiple
5. [[ev-ebitda|EV/EBITDA]] — capital-structure-neutral cousin
6. [[roe|Return on Equity]] — capital efficiency + DuPont decomposition

## Process — turning analysis into action (7–10)

7. [[evaluate-a-stock|How to Evaluate a Stock]] — 30-min checklist
8. [[build-a-thesis|Build a Thesis With Conviction]] — falsifiable, time-bound
9. [[size-a-position|How to Size a Position]] — risk-based, not gut
10. [[behavioral-biases|Behavioral Biases]] — your single biggest enemy

## What "done" looks like

You can pick a stock, articulate (in writing) why you'd own it, define what would prove you wrong, choose a position size from your risk budget, and explain the trade to a skeptic without flailing.
