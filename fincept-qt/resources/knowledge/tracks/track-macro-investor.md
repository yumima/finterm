# Path: Macro Investor

Top-down investing: rates, FX, commodities, and reading the regime before it rotates.

Estimated time: **7–10 hours of focused reading**, plus ongoing market observation.

## Who This Track Is For

You think about the economy before you think about individual stocks. You want to understand how interest rates, currency moves, and commodity cycles flow through asset prices — and how to position a portfolio around regime shifts before the majority of the market has priced them in. You may be building a macro overlay for an existing portfolio, or constructing a pure macro book.

## What "Done" Looks Like

You can look at a yield curve inversion, a widening credit spread, and declining PMI — and construct a coherent regime narrative. You can translate that narrative into specific asset class tilts: reducing equity beta, adding duration, expressing a currency view. And you can identify what would falsify your thesis.

## Key Mindset Shift

**Macro investing is about being early, not right.** If you wait for the Federal Reserve to confirm the rate cycle turn, equities have already repriced 20%. The edge in macro is in identifying the inflection *before* consensus — which requires tolerating being early and wrong before you're right. This means position sizing matters even more than directional accuracy: you need to be able to hold a correct thesis through the drawdown before it pays off.

The second shift: **regime, not prediction.** Macro investors don't forecast GDP to two decimal places. They identify which regime they're in (reflationary expansion, late cycle tightening, risk-off deleveraging) and ask which assets perform well in that regime. The question is "what regime are we in?" not "what is GDP next quarter?"
