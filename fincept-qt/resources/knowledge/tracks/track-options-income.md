# Path: Options Income

Covered calls, cash-secured puts, spreads — generating consistent yield from options without blowing up.

Estimated time: **5–8 hours of focused reading**, plus paper-trading before committing real capital.

## Who This Track Is For

You own stocks or have cash you'd like to deploy productively. You've heard that selling options can generate income and you want to understand how to do it correctly — not just mechanically, but with full understanding of the risk you're taking on and when each strategy stops making sense. This track is not for option buyers; it's for systematic premium sellers who want yield, not lottery tickets.

## What "Done" Looks Like

You can look at an option chain, identify a reasonable covered call or cash-secured put given your stock view, understand the maximum profit, maximum loss, and break-even price for the structure, and assess whether current IV makes it worth selling. You can explain to a skeptic exactly what risk you're carrying.

## Key Mindset Shift

**You are an insurance company, not a speculator.** When you sell options, you collect premium in exchange for taking on an obligation. Insurance companies profit over time by collecting more premium than they pay in claims — but individual policies can be losers. You need to think in terms of *expected value over many trades*, not the outcome of any single one.

The most common mistake in options income strategies is treating them as "low risk" because the stock doesn't need to move much to profit. That's true at the position level, but not at the portfolio level. A covered call strategy on 20 different names in the same sector means 20 correlated positions that can all go wrong together in a sector drawdown. Diversification of the underlying is as important as the option structure itself.

**IV environment determines whether to sell.** In a low-IV environment (VIX below 15), premiums are thin — you're taking meaningful risk for modest income. In a high-IV environment (VIX above 25), premiums are fat but the underlying moves are also large. The sweet spot is elevated IV in stocks where you have a genuine view and are comfortable owning at the lower price.
