**EV/Revenue** (Enterprise Value-to-Revenue) is a valuation multiple that compares a company's total enterprise value to its annual revenues — a capital-structure-neutral version of the price-to-sales ratio.

EV/Revenue is the primary valuation multiple for high-growth companies without earnings, and for cross-company comparisons where capital structures differ significantly.

## Formula

```
EV/Revenue = Enterprise Value / Annual Revenue

Enterprise Value = Market Cap + Net Debt + Preferred + Minority Interest − Cash
```

## Worked example

```
Company A (SaaS):
  Market cap: $10B
  Net debt: $500M
  EV: $10.5B
  Revenue (TTM): $700M
  EV/Revenue = $10.5B / $700M = 15×

Company B (peer SaaS):
  Market cap: $6B
  Net debt: $2B (debt-heavy)
  EV: $8B
  Revenue (TTM): $700M
  EV/Revenue = $8B / $700M = 11.4×
```

Using price-to-sales alone (ignoring debt):
  Company A: $10B / $700M = 14.3×
  Company B: $6B / $700M = 8.6× ← looks 40% cheaper but has $2B more debt

EV/Revenue correctly shows Company B is only 25% cheaper on a total-cost basis.

## EV/Revenue benchmarks by sector

| Sector | Typical EV/Revenue | Context |
|---|---|---|
| E-commerce / retail | 0.3–1.5× | Thin margins |
| Consumer discretionary | 0.5–2× | Mixed margins |
| Healthcare services | 0.5–2× | Regulated margins |
| Industrial | 1–2.5× | Stable margins |
| Pharma / biotech | 3–8× | High margin + patent value |
| Enterprise SaaS (mature) | 5–15× | 25–40% FCF margins |
| High-growth cloud | 10–30×+ | Revenue reinvested; premium for growth |
| Hypergrowth AI infrastructure | 20–50×+ | Near-term growth dominant |

## Rule-based framework: is the EV/Revenue justified?

```
Implied FCF multiple = EV/Revenue / FCF Margin
```

A company at 20× EV/Revenue with 40% expected FCF margin trades at 50× EV/FCF — aggressive but not unreasonable if growth is high. At 20× EV/Revenue with 5% margins: 400× EV/FCF — very hard to justify.

## EV/NTM Revenue vs. EV/LTM Revenue

**LTM (last twelve months)**: backward-looking; concrete; punishes growth momentum.
**NTM (next twelve months)**: forward-looking; uses revenue consensus estimate; reflects growth premium.

Most growth companies are valued on NTM revenue to avoid penalizing rapidly growing companies for their current (smaller) revenue base.

## Pitfalls

- Revenue quality matters: subscription revenue deserves a higher multiple than one-time project revenue or commoditized product revenue.
- High EV/Revenue doesn't tell you when (or if) the company will generate meaningful FCF.
- Comparing EV/Revenue across different sectors is nearly meaningless without adjusting for margin expectations.
- Revenue can be inflated by channel stuffing, aggressive recognition, or deferred revenue drawdown — check for quality signals.
