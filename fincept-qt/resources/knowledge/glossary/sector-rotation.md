**Sector rotation** is the practice of shifting portfolio allocations between stock market sectors in anticipation of or response to changes in the business cycle, macroeconomic conditions, or monetary policy.

The idea: different sectors perform best at different stages of the economic cycle. By rotating into leading sectors ahead of transitions, investors aim to outperform a static allocation.

## The economic cycle and sector leadership

| Cycle Phase | Leading Sectors | Lagging Sectors |
|---|---|---|
| Early expansion (recovery) | Financials, consumer discretionary, real estate | Utilities, consumer staples |
| Mid cycle (growth) | Technology, industrials, materials | Healthcare (defensive), utilities |
| Late cycle (slowing) | Energy, healthcare, consumer staples | Financials, consumer discretionary |
| Recession | Utilities, consumer staples, healthcare | Energy, materials, industrials |

This framework is generalized — cycles don't follow scripts cleanly.

## Worked example — 2022 rotation

```
2022 context: Rising rates, falling equities, supply shock

Winner sectors:
  Energy (XLE):    +63.4% (oil shock + supply deficit)
  Consumer Staples (XLP): −2.0% (defensive; minimal drawdown)

Loser sectors:
  Technology (XLK): −28.3% (rate-sensitive, long-duration)
  Consumer Disc (XLY): −37.1% (AMZN + TSLA implosion)
  Communication (XLC): −40.7% (GOOG, META correction)

Investor who rotated from Tech → Energy in Jan 2022:
  Avoided −28% in Tech, captured +63% in Energy → ~90% relative swing
```

## Rate sensitivity of sectors

A critical rotation driver is the direction and level of interest rates:

| Rates Rising | Sectors | Rates Falling | Sectors |
|---|---|---|---|
| Positive for | Financials (bank margins) | Positive for | Utilities, REITs (bond proxies) |
| Positive for | Energy (nominal hedge) | Positive for | Tech (growth premium expands) |
| Negative for | Utilities, REITs (yield competition) | Negative for | Financials (NIM compression) |

## Factor rotation vs. sector rotation

Related concepts:
- **Factor rotation**: rotating between value, growth, quality, momentum factors.
- **Geographic rotation**: shifting between U.S., international developed, and emerging markets.
- **Asset class rotation**: bonds vs. equities vs. real assets.

All follow similar business cycle logic.

## Pitfalls

- Cycle timing is notoriously difficult — sectors often lead the cycle, meaning by the time rotation is "obvious," much of the move has already occurred.
- Business cycles don't have fixed lengths; the 2020 cycle was the shortest on record (2 months).
- Sector ETFs contain heterogeneous stocks — XLY includes both McDonald's (defensive) and Expedia (highly cyclical).
- Frequent rotation generates tax events and transaction costs that can eliminate the alpha generated.
