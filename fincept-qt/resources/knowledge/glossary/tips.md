**TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities)** are U.S. government bonds whose principal is adjusted for inflation, guaranteeing that returns keep pace with the CPI.

The defining feature: the principal rises with inflation (measured by CPI-U) and falls with deflation, but never below the original face value at maturity. The coupon rate is fixed, but because it applies to an adjusted principal, the dollar coupon payment grows with inflation.

## Formula

```
Adjusted Principal = Face Value × (Current CPI / Base CPI)
Coupon Payment     = Fixed Coupon Rate × Adjusted Principal
```

## Worked example — 10-year TIPS

```
Face value:          $10,000
Fixed coupon:        1.5%
Base CPI (issue):    280.0
CPI after 1 year:    288.0  (+2.86% inflation)
Adjusted principal:  $10,000 × (288.0 / 280.0) = $10,286
Annual coupon:       1.5% × $10,286 = $154.29
```

A nominal Treasury at 4.5% coupon would pay $450. TIPS pays less in nominal dollars but provides a real return guarantee.

## Real yield vs nominal yield

The TIPS yield is a **real yield** — the return above inflation. The difference between the nominal Treasury yield and the TIPS yield is the **breakeven inflation rate**:

```
Breakeven Inflation = Nominal Treasury Yield − TIPS Real Yield
                    = 4.50% − 1.80% = 2.70%
```

If actual inflation exceeds 2.70%, TIPS outperforms the nominal bond. If inflation comes in below 2.70%, the nominal bond wins.

## When TIPS make sense

| Market condition | TIPS | Nominal Bonds |
|---|---|---|
| Inflation > breakeven | Outperforms | Underperforms |
| Inflation < breakeven | Underperforms | Outperforms |
| Deflation | Principal protected at face | Coupon stays fixed |
| Rate hike cycle | Duration risk still present | Same duration risk |

## Practical considerations

- TIPS are sold at auction and trade actively; 5yr, 10yr, and 30yr are most liquid.
- Inflation adjustments are taxable in the year they occur even if not received — the "phantom income" problem for taxable accounts. TIPS are most efficient in tax-deferred accounts (IRA, 401k).
- TIPS ETFs (e.g., TIP, SCHP) distribute monthly; they suffer from the same duration risk as nominal bond funds.

## Pitfalls

- Negative real yields (2020–2022) mean investors pay above inflation for safety — not a great deal for new buyers.
- CPI-U may not reflect your personal inflation basket; TIPS hedge the index, not your actual cost of living.
- Deflation scenario: principal falls but never below original face, so you're protected at maturity — not necessarily if you sell early.
