**Dollar-cost averaging (DCA)** is the investment strategy of investing a fixed dollar amount at regular intervals — regardless of price — rather than investing a lump sum all at once.

DCA is widely used in retirement accounts (401k automatic contributions), brokerage automations, and crypto accumulation. It is primarily a behavioral tool that removes the decision of timing the market from the investment process.

## Mechanics

```
Example: Invest $500/month into S&P 500 ETF

Month 1: SPY at $500 → buy 1.00 shares
Month 2: SPY at $450 → buy 1.11 shares (more shares for same $)
Month 3: SPY at $480 → buy 1.04 shares
Month 4: SPY at $520 → buy 0.96 shares
Month 5: SPY at $550 → buy 0.91 shares

Total invested: $2,500
Total shares: 5.02
Average cost: $2,500 / 5.02 = $498.00/share

vs. Average market price over 5 months: ($500+$450+$480+$520+$550)/5 = $500
```

DCA average cost ($498) is slightly below the average price ($500) — because more shares are purchased when prices are lower.

## DCA vs. lump sum investing

Academic research consistently shows lump sum investing outperforms DCA approximately 2/3 of the time in trending bull markets (because money is invested earlier when prices are lower on average):

| Market Condition | Better Strategy |
|---|---|
| Trending bull market | Lump sum (typically) |
| Range-bound / volatile | DCA reduces average cost |
| Declining then recovering | DCA significantly outperforms |
| Continuously declining | Both lose; DCA loses less |

**But** — lump sum requires having the full amount available and the emotional fortitude to invest it all at once. For most regular investors building wealth from income, DCA is the only practical option.

## The behavioral case for DCA

- Removes market timing anxiety ("should I wait for a pullback?")
- Creates consistent investment habit regardless of market conditions.
- Prevents paralysis during volatile markets.
- Automates the savings → investment pipeline.

## DCA in bear markets — the accumulation advantage

```
Scenario: Stock falls 40% over 12 months, then recovers fully

Lump sum ($12,000 at start):
  All at peak → needs 67% gain just to recover to break-even

DCA ($1,000/month for 12 months, averaging down):
  Average cost ~30% below peak (buying cheaper each month)
  Needs only ~43% gain to recover
  → Recovers much faster once market turns
```

## Limitations

- DCA does NOT guarantee a profit or protect against loss.
- In a secular bull market, waiting to invest "gradually" costs returns compared to immediate lump sum.
- For large one-time amounts (inheritance, asset sale proceeds), DCA over 6–12 months is a reasonable psychological compromise — not an optimal strategy.

## Crypto DCA

DCA is especially popular in crypto due to extreme volatility. Many investors automate daily or weekly BTC purchases regardless of price:

```
Daily BTC purchase: $50/day
  Bull market: average cost near highs
  Bear market: average cost near lows
  Multi-cycle: average cost significantly below cycle peaks
```

## Pitfalls

- DCA into a declining asset (individual stock, sector) doesn't improve outcome — diversification of timing is not diversification of risk.
- "Automatic investing" can become mindless; periodic review of what you're buying is still necessary.
- High transaction costs on small recurring purchases can erode the cost-averaging benefit (now largely solved by zero-commission brokers).
