A **leveraged buyout (LBO)** is the acquisition of a company using a significant amount of borrowed money (leverage) to meet most of the purchase price, with the acquired company's assets and cash flows typically serving as collateral for the loans.

LBOs are the primary deal structure for private equity firms. The leverage amplifies equity returns if the thesis works out — and amplifies losses if it doesn't. The art of LBO investing is finding companies that can safely carry debt while improving operationally.

## Structure

```
Total acquisition price: $1,000M

Funded by:
  Equity (PE sponsor):   $300M  (30%)
  Senior secured debt:   $500M  (50%)
  Mezzanine/2nd lien:    $200M  (20%)

Closing leverage: 7× EBITDA (assuming $100M EBITDA, $700M total debt)
```

## Return drivers

LBO returns come from three sources:

1. **Earnings growth** — improve EBITDA through revenue growth or margin expansion.
2. **Multiple expansion** — exit at a higher EV/EBITDA multiple than entry.
3. **Debt paydown** — as the company pays down debt, equity value grows even if EBITDA is flat.

```
Entry:  $1B enterprise value, $700M debt, $300M equity
Exit (5yr): EBITDA grows from $100M to $150M; exit at 9× EBITDA
  Exit EV:  $1,350M
  Debt:     $400M (paid down)
  Exit equity: $1,350M − $400M = $950M

Return: $950M / $300M = 3.17× MOIC
IRR: ~26% over 5 years
```

## Ideal LBO candidate characteristics

| Characteristic | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Stable, recurring cash flows | Reliably services debt |
| Low capex requirements | More FCF available for debt paydown |
| Market leadership / pricing power | Protects margins under leverage |
| Fragmented competitors | Acquisition bolt-on opportunities |
| Non-cyclical industry | Reduces default risk in downturns |
| Clean balance sheet pre-LBO | Maximizes available debt capacity |

## Key LBO metrics

- **MOIC (Multiple of Invested Capital)**: Exit equity / Entry equity. Target: 2.5–4.0×.
- **IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**: Annualized return. Target: 20–25%+.
- **Entry multiple**: EV / EBITDA at purchase. Lower is better.
- **Exit multiple**: EV / EBITDA at sale. Higher is better.
- **Leverage multiple**: Total debt / EBITDA. Typically 5–8× in normal markets.

## Debt components in an LBO

| Tranche | Seniority | Typical Rate | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revolver (RCF) | Senior secured | SOFR + 200–300 bps | 1st |
| Term Loan A/B | Senior secured | SOFR + 300–450 bps | 1st–2nd |
| High-yield bonds | Senior unsecured | Fixed, 6–10% | 3rd |
| Mezzanine / PIK | Subordinated | 12–15%+ | Last (before equity) |

## Pitfalls

- High leverage amplifies downside just as much as upside — a 20% EBITDA decline can wipe out equity entirely.
- Covenant violations can trigger lender acceleration even before cash flow insolvency.
- PE sponsors often extract dividends via "dividend recaps" (borrowing more to pay themselves), increasing leverage and risk for remaining debtholders.
- LBO valuation is highly cyclical — multiples paid at cycle peaks often compress significantly by exit time.
