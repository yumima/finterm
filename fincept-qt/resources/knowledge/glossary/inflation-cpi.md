**CPI (Consumer Price Index)** is the primary measure of inflation in the United States — tracking the average price change of a representative basket of consumer goods and services over time, published monthly by the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).

Inflation measures how quickly purchasing power erodes. It directly impacts central bank policy, bond yields, real returns on all assets, and the cost of living.

## CPI basket composition (approximate U.S. weights)

| Category | Weight | Key Items |
|---|---|---|
| Housing (shelter) | 33% | Rent, owners' equivalent rent |
| Food | 14% | Groceries, restaurants |
| Energy | 7% | Gasoline, electricity |
| Medical care | 7% | Doctor visits, prescription drugs |
| Transportation | 6% | New/used cars, auto insurance |
| Apparel | 3% | Clothing, footwear |
| Recreation | 6% | Entertainment, sporting goods |
| Other | 24% | Education, personal care, etc. |

## CPI variants

| Measure | Definition | Fed Use? |
|---|---|---|
| **CPI (headline)** | All items including food and energy | Reference; not primary |
| **Core CPI** | Ex food and energy | Widely cited; not primary |
| **PCE (headline)** | Broader consumption basket | Primary Fed target |
| **Core PCE** | PCE ex food and energy | Fed's primary gauge |
| **PCEPI** | Chain-weighted; adjusts for substitution | More accurate than CPI |

CPI tends to run ~20–30 bps above PCE due to different weighting methodologies (shelter has larger CPI weight).

## Formula

```
CPI Inflation Rate = (CPI_current − CPI_prior) / CPI_prior × 100

Month-over-month (MoM): Compares to previous month
Year-over-year (YoY): Compares to same month last year
```

## Worked example

```
CPI (May 2026): 314.2
CPI (May 2025): 300.8
CPI (Apr 2026): 313.6

YoY inflation: (314.2 − 300.8) / 300.8 = 4.45%
MoM inflation: (314.2 − 313.6) / 313.6 = 0.19% = 2.3% annualized
```

## How inflation affects asset classes

| Asset | Inflation Effect |
|---|---|
| Cash / short-term bonds | Negative (real value erodes) |
| Long-duration bonds | Very negative (value destroyed) |
| TIPS | Positive (principal adjusts) |
| Equities | Mixed (pricing power matters) |
| Commodities | Positive (often cause of inflation) |
| Real estate | Positive (hard asset) |
| Gold | Generally positive |

## Inflation regimes

| Regime | Definition | Policy Response |
|---|---|---|
| Deflation | CPI < 0% | Aggressive easing |
| Low inflation | 0–2% | Accommodative |
| Target | ~2% | Neutral |
| Elevated | 2–5% | Tightening |
| High inflation | 5–10% | Aggressive tightening |
| Hyperinflation | > 50%/month | Crisis |

## Base effects

YoY inflation is influenced by "base effects" — if prices spiked sharply 12 months ago, the current YoY reading will be depressed even if prices are still rising MoM.

## Pitfalls

- CPI is an average — your personal inflation may differ significantly based on housing cost, commuting, healthcare, and consumption patterns.
- Shelter inflation in CPI lags actual market rents by 12–18 months (it uses leases, not new contract rents), making CPI inflation stickier in both directions.
- Seasonally adjusted vs. unadjusted CPI can confuse; financial media sometimes mixes them.
