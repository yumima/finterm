**Smart beta** (also called factor investing or strategic beta) is an investment approach that uses rules-based, transparent strategies to systematically tilt a portfolio toward well-documented risk factors or investment characteristics, aiming to outperform traditional market-cap-weighted indexes.

It sits between passive indexing and active management: transparent and rule-based like indexing, but purposely deviating from market-cap weights like active management.

## Common smart beta factors

| Factor | Definition | Academic Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Value** | Low price relative to fundamentals (P/B, P/E, P/S) | Fama-French (1993) |
| **Momentum** | Past winners continue to outperform | Jegadeesh-Titman (1993) |
| **Quality** | High ROE, low debt, stable earnings | Novy-Marx (2013) |
| **Low volatility** | Low historical volatility or beta | Black (1972) |
| **Size** | Small-cap premium | Fama-French (1993) |
| **Dividend yield** | High dividend payers | Gordon, various |

## Why factors deliver premium (two views)

**Risk-based**: Factors are compensation for bearing systematic risk. Value stocks are cheap because they are financially distressed; their premium compensates for that risk.

**Behavioral-based**: Investors systematically misprice based on cognitive biases. Momentum works because markets underreact; value works because markets overreact.

## Worked example — value vs. growth, 2022

```
iShares MSCI USA Value (IUSV):  +5.3%
iShares MSCI USA Growth (IWF):  −29.2%

Spread: +34.5 percentage points (value factor payoff year)
```

Value underperformed for ~10 years (2010–2020); then significantly outperformed in 2022 when rates rose sharply and growth stocks' long-duration valuations corrected.

## Factor timing and cycles

Factors are not consistently outperforming — they rotate:

| Period | Dominant Factor | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 2010–2020 | Growth / momentum | Low rates favored long-duration assets |
| 2022 | Value / low vol | Rate shock hit long-duration stocks |
| 2023–2024 | Momentum / growth | AI narrative; mega-cap dominance |

Attempting to time factors is difficult; persistent multi-factor diversification is more robust.

## Smart beta vs. active management

| Feature | Passive Index | Smart Beta | Active |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rules-based | Yes | Yes | No |
| Market-cap weighted | Yes | No | No |
| Fee level | Very low | Low-moderate | High |
| Benchmark | Market cap | Factor exposure | Manager-defined |
| Transparency | Full | Full | Limited |

## Pitfalls

- Factor premia can be crowded out — as more money chases a factor, the valuation spread narrows and future expected returns decline.
- Factor "zoo" problem: hundreds of claimed factors, many the result of data mining rather than genuine economic rationale.
- Some factors (notably value) can have multi-year underperformance that tests investor discipline.
- Smart beta ETF costs may exceed pure index ETFs by 3–10× (0.15–0.40% vs 0.03–0.05%).
