**Investment grade** is a credit rating designation indicating that a bond issuer has a relatively low risk of default — rated BBB-/Baa3 or above by S&P/Fitch and Moody's respectively — making the bonds eligible for purchase by conservative institutional investors with regulatory constraints.

Investment-grade bonds sit on the "safer" end of the credit spectrum, offering lower yields than high-yield bonds in exchange for lower default risk and broader institutional ownership.

## Credit rating scale

| Rating (S&P/Fitch) | Rating (Moody's) | Category |
|---|---|---|
| AAA | Aaa | Highest quality |
| AA+, AA, AA- | Aa1, Aa2, Aa3 | Very high quality |
| A+, A, A- | A1, A2, A3 | Upper medium grade |
| BBB+, BBB, BBB- | Baa1, Baa2, Baa3 | Lower medium grade (IG floor) |
| BB+, BB, BB- | Ba1, Ba2, Ba3 | Non-investment grade / speculative |
| B+, B, B- | B1, B2, B3 | Highly speculative |
| CCC and below | Caa and below | Substantial default risk |
| D | D | In default |

BBB- / Baa3 is the **investment-grade floor** — the dividing line between IG and high yield.

## Why the IG/HY distinction matters structurally

Many institutional investors — insurance companies, pension funds, bank trust departments — are legally or by charter restricted to investment-grade only:

```
U.S. pension plan:
  Mandate: No securities rated below BBB- (IG only)
  Effect: Downgrade from BBB- to BB+ triggers mandatory selling
  → "Fallen angel" — name for IG bonds downgraded to HY
  → Fallen angels experience forced selling regardless of intrinsic value
```

This creates specific market dynamics — fallen angels are often mispriced at downgrade and can be attractive to HY-mandate investors.

## Investment-grade default rates

IG bonds have very low historical default rates:

| Rating | 5-year cumulative default rate |
|---|---|
| AAA | < 0.1% |
| AA | ~0.3% |
| A | ~0.7% |
| BBB | ~2.5% |
| BB | ~10% |
| B | ~25% |
| CCC | ~50%+ |

## Investment-grade bond market

The U.S. IG corporate bond market (Bloomberg US Corporate Bond Index) represents ~$10+ trillion in outstanding debt. Major issuers:
- Apple, Microsoft, Amazon (AAA-range)
- JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs (A-range)
- Ford, AT&T (BBB-range)

## IG spread dynamics

Investment-grade spreads to Treasuries reflect economic conditions:
- **Tight spreads (50–80 bps for IG index)**: Late cycle complacency, strong economic conditions.
- **Moderate spreads (100–150 bps)**: Normal risk pricing.
- **Wide spreads (200–300+ bps)**: Recession fears, credit stress.

## Pitfalls

- BBB is the largest component of IG indices and is most at risk of downgrade to HY in recessions — the "BBB cliff" risk.
- Investment-grade designation doesn't mean zero default risk — IG bonds can and do default (Enron, Lehman Brothers were IG-rated shortly before failure).
- Ratings lag fundamental deterioration; bonds may deserve junk status before agencies act.
