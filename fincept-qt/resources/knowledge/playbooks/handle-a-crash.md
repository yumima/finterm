# How to Handle a Market Crash

A crash will happen. Statistically, a 20%+ bear market every 7–10 years; a 30%+ every 15–20. Most portfolios survive in absolute terms. **Most investors don't survive psychologically** — they panic-sell the bottom and miss the recovery.

This playbook covers three phases: pre-crash preparation, in-the-moment discipline, post-crash positioning.

## Phase 1 — Pre-crash preparation (do this BEFORE you need it)

The decisions you make under no stress determine how you'll behave under maximum stress.

### The cash runway

```
Liquid cash buffer = 6–12 months of expenses
Held outside investment portfolio
In MMF / T-bills / HYSA
```

This is not part of your investment allocation. It's pre-decided withdrawal protection.

### Pre-committed action plan

Write down — in advance — what you'll do at various drawdown thresholds:

```
Portfolio drops:    Action
−10%                Review; do nothing
−20%                Rebalance toward target (sell winners → buy losers)
−30%                Add 25% of cash buffer to risk assets
−40%                Add another 25% of cash buffer
−50%                Aggressively rebalance to overweight equity
```

This converts panic into mechanical action.

### Stress-test the portfolio

What happens to your specific holdings at:
- −20% market drop?
- −40% market drop?
- 100bp unexpected rate rise?
- Currency crisis?

If any single scenario produces a loss you couldn't sustain, **you're sized too big now** — fix it before the storm.

### Insurance considerations

For meaningful portfolios:
- **Long-dated put options** on broad index (1–2% portfolio cost; protects against tail moves)
- **VIX call options** (smaller bets; pay off in vol spikes)
- **Long-duration Treasuries** (some) — flight to quality bid in crashes (not always)
- **Gold** (5–10% allocation) — historic crash hedge

## Phase 2 — In-the-moment (when it's actually happening)

### What NOT to do

- **Don't panic-sell** — locks in the loss permanently
- **Don't try to call the bottom** — you'll miss it
- **Don't go to 100% cash** — you'll never re-enter
- **Don't add leverage** — averaging down with margin is how blowups happen
- **Don't read finance Twitter** — it's all panic and bottom-callers
- **Don't make any decision while feeling intense emotion** — wait 24h

### What to do

- **Re-read your pre-crash plan** — execute it mechanically
- **Honor existing stops** if they're hit; don't widen
- **Rebalance** to target if you've drifted (sell what's holding up, buy what's fallen)
- **Tax-loss harvest** in taxable accounts (replace with similar-but-not-identical ETF)
- **Verify your cash buffer is intact** — that's the source of resilience
- **Step away from screens** — daily monitoring during a crash is psychologically destructive

### Mental model

The price you see today is not "what your investment is worth permanently" — it's what one specific buyer would pay you in this minute. Long-term value of cash-flow-generating businesses doesn't drop 30% in three weeks (even if the price does).

## Phase 3 — Post-crash positioning

Markets bottom **before** the recession officially ends. By the time data confirms recovery, the rally is half over.

### Recovery patterns

| Scenario | Recovery shape |
|---|---|
| Sharp + brief (COVID Mar 2020) | V-shape; recover in months |
| Severe + prolonged (GFC 2008) | U-shape; 2–4 year recovery |
| Stagnation (2000–2002) | L-shape; multi-year sideways then break |
| Long bear (1970s) | Flat-to-down for 7+ years before next bull |

### How to add risk back

**Don't try to nail the bottom**. Spread additions over time:

```
You decided pre-crash to deploy 50% of cash buffer.

Bad: try to deploy all $25,000 on "the bottom day" → never hit
Good: deploy $5,000 every 2 weeks for 10 weeks
        Average price probably better than waiting for clarity
        Captures most of recovery if rally starts
        Limits regret if it falls further
```

Dollar-cost averaging on the way down works the same.

## Worked example — what 60/40 looked like in 2020

```
Pre-COVID portfolio: $500k (60% stocks, 40% bonds)
  $300k SPY
  $200k IEF (intermediate Treasuries)

March 2020 panic:
  SPY: $300k → $200k (−33%)
  IEF: $200k → $215k (+7%, flight to quality)
  Total: $500k → $415k (−17%)

Pre-committed plan: at −15%, rebalance toward 60/40

Rebalance:
  Target stocks at 60% of $415k = $249k
  Sell $34k bonds → buy $34k SPY
  Buys SPY at $200k effective basis (down vs $300k)

By end of 2020:
  SPY: $234k (back near pre-COVID)
  IEF: $185k
  Total: $419k

vs not rebalancing:
  SPY: $216k (smaller position rallied less in absolute terms)
  IEF: $215k
  Total: $431k

Looks "worse" — but rebalancing forced buying low, which pays over multiple cycles.
```

## Famous crash mistakes

- **Selling Mar 23, 2020** (the bottom day) — never re-bought
- **Going to all cash in 2008** and missing the 2009–11 doubling
- **"Just one more lower-low"** in 2002 → eventually capitulated near bottom
- **Adding leverage at peaks** ("buy the dip") that turned into much bigger dips
- **Switching strategies during a drawdown** (chasing what's been working in the new regime)

## Decision rules

- **Cash buffer (6–12 mo expenses)** is non-negotiable
- **Pre-committed action plan** before next crisis
- **Rebalance threshold trigger** during the crash; mechanical execution
- **No discretionary action under intense emotion** — 24h cooling off
- **Don't try to time the bottom** — DCA in over weeks
- **Avoid leverage** during a crash; preserve flexibility
